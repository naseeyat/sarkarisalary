Educational Qualification

Flying Branch

  • Mandatory: 50% marks each in Physics & Maths at 10+2 level
  • Graduation (3 years) in any discipline with 60% marks, OR
  • BE/B.Tech (4 years) with 60% marks, OR
  • Section A & B of AMIE/AMASI with 60% marks

Ground Duty Technical - AE(L)

  • Mandatory: 50% marks each in Physics & Maths at 10+2 level
  • B.Tech/BE (4 years) in specified streams with 60% marks
  • Streams: Electronics, Computer Science, Electrical, Instrumentation, etc.

Ground Duty Technical - AE(M)

  • Mandatory: 50% marks each in Physics & Maths at 10+2 level
  • B.Tech/BE (4 years) in specified streams with 60% marks
  • Streams: Aerospace, Mechanical, Automobile, Production, etc.

Ground Duty Non-Technical

  • Weapon Systems: Graduation (60% marks) + 50% in Physics & Maths at 10+2
  • Administration & Logistics: Graduation (60% marks)
  • Accounts: B.Com with 60% marks OR CA/ICWA/CS
  • Education: Post-graduation in relevant subject with 50% marks
  • Meteorology: Physics/Maths/Statistics/Computer Science with 50% marks

Marital Status

Only unmarried candidates are eligible. Widows without encumbrance are also eligible.

üìù SELECTION PROCESS

Stage 1: AFCAT Online Examination

  • Date: 31 January 2026 (Saturday)
  • Mode: Online Computer-Based Test (CBT)
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Total Marks: 300
  • Sections: General Awareness, Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, Reasoning

Stage 2: Air Force Selection Board (AFSB) Testing

Candidates shortlisted from AFCAT will be called for AFSB testing (5 days):

  • Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) Tests
  • Psychological Tests
  • Group Testing Officer (GTO) Tasks
  • Personal Interview
  • Conference

Stage 3: Medical Examination

Candidates recommended by AFSB undergo comprehensive medical examination at designated military hospitals.

Stage 4: Final Merit List

Final selection based on combined performance in AFCAT, AFSB, and medical fitness.
